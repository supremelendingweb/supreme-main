Frequently Asked Questions

General answers to the questions we hear most. For your specific scenario, please connect with a licensed loan officer.

Getting Started

What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is typically based on stated information. Pre-approval involves submitted documentation and a credit review and is generally a stronger indicator to sellers. Neither is a commitment to lend.

How much down payment do I need?

It varies by program and borrower profile. Some loans, like VA and USDA, may allow $0 down for eligible borrowers. Conventional and FHA programs have minimum down payment requirements that vary.

What documents will I need?

Common items include 30 days of pay stubs, two years of W-2s and tax returns, two months of asset/bank statements, and a photo ID. Self-employed borrowers may need additional business documentation.

About Rates & Costs

Can you tell me what my interest rate will be?

Rates change throughout the day and depend on factors specific to your loan. We don't quote rates on this site — your loan officer will provide a personalized quote.

What are closing costs?

Closing costs are the fees and charges paid at closing, including items like origination, title, recording, and prepaid taxes and insurance. They appear on your Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure.

Programs & Eligibility

Which loan program is best for me?

It depends on your goals, income, credit, down payment, property type, and location. A loan officer can help you compare programs based on your situation.

I'm a veteran. How do I use my VA benefit?

You'll typically need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE). Your loan officer can help you request one and walk you through eligibility, entitlement, and program rules.

The Process

How long does the process take?

Timelines vary based on borrower responsiveness, property type, appraisal turn times, and underwriting conditions. Your loan officer will set expectations for your scenario.

What is an appraisal?

An appraisal is an independent licensed appraiser's estimate of a property's market value, used by the lender to evaluate the collateral.

Privacy & Security

How do you protect my information?

We treat your information as confidential nonpublic personal information under applicable privacy laws. Please do not send sensitive items like Social Security numbers via standard email; ask your loan officer about secure document upload.
