Mortgage Dictionary

Mortgages have a vocabulary of their own. Here are plain-language definitions for the terms you'll encounter most often.

A

Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM): A mortgage whose interest rate adjusts periodically based on a published index plus a margin.

Amortization: The process of paying off a loan over time through regular payments of principal and interest.

Annual Percentage Rate (APR): The cost of credit expressed as a yearly rate, including the interest rate and certain financing charges.

Appraisal: A licensed appraiser's estimate of a property's market value.

C

Closing Costs: Fees paid at closing such as origination, title, recording, and prepaid items.

Closing Disclosure (CD): A federally required disclosure detailing final loan terms and closing costs.

Conforming Loan: A loan that meets the limits and underwriting guidelines of Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac.

D

Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI): The ratio of monthly debt payments to gross monthly income; lenders use this to assess capacity.

Down Payment: The portion of the purchase price the buyer pays in cash up front.

E

Earnest Money: A deposit submitted with a purchase offer to demonstrate good faith.

Escrow: An account a lender uses to hold funds for taxes and insurance, paid as part of the monthly mortgage payment.

F

FHA Loan: A mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration with flexible qualification standards.

Fixed-Rate Mortgage: A mortgage with an interest rate that stays the same for the full term.

L

Loan Estimate (LE): A federally required disclosure provided after application that summarizes loan terms and projected costs.

Loan-to-Value (LTV): The loan amount divided by the property value, expressed as a percentage.

P

PITI: Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance — the four core components of most mortgage payments.

Pre-Approval: A lender's conditional indication of how much you may be able to borrow based on documentation reviewed.

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI): Insurance often required on conventional loans with less than 20% down.

R

Refinance: Replacing an existing loan with a new loan, typically to change rate, term, or access equity.

U

Underwriting: The process by which a lender evaluates a borrower's credit, income, assets, and the property to make a credit decision.

USDA Loan: A mortgage program supporting eligible rural and suburban properties and incomes.

V

VA Loan: A mortgage benefit for eligible service members, veterans, and surviving spouses, supported by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs.
