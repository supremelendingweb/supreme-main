The Mortgage Process, Step by Step

Every borrower's journey is a little different, but most loans move through these six phases. Your loan officer will tailor the timeline to your situation.

1. Pre-Approval

Your loan officer reviews income, assets, credit, and goals to estimate how much you may be able to borrow. Pre-approval is conditional and not a commitment to lend.

2. Home Shopping

You and your real estate agent identify properties within your pre-approval. Your loan officer is available to support offers and answer scenario questions.

3. Application

Once you're under contract, you'll complete a full mortgage application and receive a Loan Estimate (LE) within three business days.

4. Processing

Your processor verifies documentation, orders the appraisal, title work, and prepares the file for underwriting.

5. Underwriting

An underwriter reviews credit, capacity, collateral, and program eligibility, then issues a decision. Conditions may be requested before final approval.

6. Closing

You'll receive the Closing Disclosure (CD) at least three business days before closing. At signing, the loan funds and the home becomes yours.
